## Selected Descent

Descent 002 has the strongest law-per-decision ratio in the queue: 101
operation roots share a depth-one grammar and the current red surface is
bounded. Its closed destination is summarized in `frame.md`, evidenced in
`ledger.md`, and ruled in four separate decision records.

The descent begins from positive law, not the current majority shape:

- operation roots require `contract.ts`, an assembly-only `index.ts`,
  `rules/index.ts`, and `strategies/index.ts` plus at least one named strategy;
- `rules/index.ts` is export-only and may be empty when no named rule exists;
- nested rule and strategy modules are local-source leaves;
- operation-local `types.ts`, `policy/`, helper buckets, and other catchalls
  have no destination in the target topology;
- generic dependency authority rejects private cross-domain and private
  sibling-operation reaches without maintaining a path inventory;
- behavior, configuration, recipes, runtime ownership, and public imports must
  remain unchanged.

Current paths are evidence. The first A.2 execution act is to assert the law in
advisory mode and re-derive the row corpus before any migration is authorized.

## Split Law

The final readiness tip is the common Graphite parent. A.2 and the independent
product continuation open as ordinary sibling children. If the continuation
later gains a real A.2 dependency, move its first unique branch above the A.2
tip with native Graphite operations; otherwise the tracks remain parallel.

This roadmap does not authorize A.2 implementation. It contains no live
violation corpus, destination registry, agent slate, or closure claim.
