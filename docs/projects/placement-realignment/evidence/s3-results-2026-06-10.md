### E2.7 structural exception (recorded, not faked)

RESOURCE_SILVER plans 6–8 against `expectedCountRange` [16,18,20] on 4 of 5
seeds (seed 1341 in range). Root cause is map capacity, not the planner: the
mock policy surface grants silver only **16 legal tiles** map-wide at 84x54
(silver's `Resource_ValidPlacements` rows are tundra-biome-bound and this
map's biome projection produces little tundra), and a 16-count floor cannot
fit on 16 tiles under the same-type spacing floor (3). The shortfall is typed
(`spacing-floor-preserved`) in the plan artifact and `belowMinWithoutShortfall`
is 0. Remediations are out of S3 scope: (a) upstream ecology/biome tundra
coverage, (b) corpus range recalibration by amendment, (c) live-engine
legality may admit more tiles than the mock emulation — Milestone A measures
this (E4.4).

Silver also drives the E2.2 recorded-shortfall arm on seeds where one
hemisphere lacks spaced gold-legal tiles; both shortfall families surface in
`resourcePlan.perType[].shortfalls` and `resourcePlan.regionMinimums[]`.

### E1.8 regression (recorded, assigned to S4)

Start placement code is unchanged in S3; the shift is an input interaction:
`plan-starts` scores candidates by placed-resource support
(`resourceSupportWeight` default 1, radius 4), and resources now concentrate
in real habitat (arid rangelands, orogenic belts, tropics) instead of a
type-blind uniform scatter. Resource hotspots in climate-extreme zones pull
starts toward them. The correcting lever is start-side (climate screening /
support-weight rebalance / E1.8-aware scoring), which is S4's write set
(starts vertical realignment); S3 must not reach into start scoring. Recorded
here and in the S3 proposal as an explicit open item for S4; E3.1/E3.2 (start
support floor/equity) remain S5 scope.
