# Strategy — What To Choose

How to make *sound* Civ7 decisions, not just legal ones. Use this when picking
research, civics, production, settle sites, and path focus. Civ7 mechanics shift
with patches — **trust in-game tooltips and the live reads over any exact number
here.**

## Default policy (follow when unsteered)

If the human gives no specific direction, play a competent builder game:

1. **Commit to the Science Legacy Path** in Antiquity (collect Codices via
   Mastery techs). It is the cleanest path to drive from the CLI and sets up a
   Science arc across Ages.
2. **Expand to — not past — the settlement cap** (starts at 4). Found towns on
   fresh-water / high-food / resource-rich plots that `settlement-recommendations`
   ranks highly.
3. **Keep everything productive:** no idle unit, no city building nothing, no
   unspent research/civic slot.
4. **Spend, don't hoard:** Influence on befriending Independent Powers and
   endeavors; Gold on buildings/Town→City; Attribute points into the Scientific
   (or path-matching) tree as they arrive.
5. **Assign every new Resource** to a settlement slot (City resources → Cities).
6. **Don't start wars.** Defend, fortify borders, accept reasonable diplomacy.
7. Re-evaluate the path each Age based on what the leader/civ is good at.

## Game shape

- Three Ages in sequence: **Antiquity → Exploration → Modern** (~150–200 turns
  each on Standard). The Age progress meter fills each turn + on big actions;
  when full, the Age ends for everyone.
- **Crisis events** hit near the end of Antiquity and Exploration (revolts,
  plague, invasion, etc.) and force you to slot negative Crisis Policies. There
  is **no crisis in the Modern Age**. Survive them, don't expect to avoid them.
- **Carries across Ages:** Leader + Attribute points, **Commanders** (with XP),
  Wonders, Ageless buildings, **Traditions**. **Resets:** tech tree, civics tree,
  Legacy progress, age-specific military units (deleted), most map resources,
  normal Social Policies. Cities tend to revert toward Towns unless a Golden Age
  legacy preserves them.
- **Leader is fixed all game; you pick a new Civ each Age.** Plan a 3-Age arc
  around the leader's attribute focus.
- **Winning:** each Age has 4 Legacy Paths with milestones banking Legacy Points;
  the game is decided in the **Modern Age** (one victory condition per path, or a
  Score Victory if the Modern meter fills). Always watch the Modern progress
  clock.

## Legacy Paths (the win engine)

Four concurrent per Age: **Science, Culture, Economic, Military.** Each has ~3
milestones → Legacy Points + bonuses; completing a path → a Golden Age legacy
choice for the next Age. Pick a focus by ~turn 15–20 and steer toward it.

| Path | Antiquity focus | Exploration focus | Modern (→ victory) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Science | Collect ~10 Codices (Mastery techs) | High-yield specialist districts | Space Race projects |
| Culture | Build Wonders (~7) | Display Relics | Collect Artifacts → World's Fair |
| Economic | Collect/assign ~20 Resources | Distant-lands Treasure fleets | Railroad/factory output → World Bank |
| Military | ~12 Settlements (conquest counts double) | Distant-lands conquest points | Ideology/conquest → Manhattan Project |

Golden Age legacies are the highest-leverage transition choice (e.g. Economic's
"keep cities as cities" skips Town reversion).

## Settlements & the cap

- **Settlement cap starts at 4.** Each settlement over the cap applies a
  Happiness penalty to **every** settlement (now uncapped) — going over tanks the
  whole empire. Raise the cap via techs/civics/Expansionist attributes before
  expanding further.
- **Towns** have no build queue: production → Gold, surplus Food → connected
  Cities. **Cities** build units/buildings/wonders with Production. New
  settlements start as Towns; convert Town→City with Gold once it is worth it
  (put Cities where you want production/wonders).
- **Town Focus** (chosen ~pop 7, permanent for the Age): Growing, Farming,
  Mining, Trade Outpost, Urban Center, Hub, Fort, etc. Match focus to terrain and
  role.
- Two buildings on an urban tile form a **Quarter** (adjacency + specialists);
  two same-civ uniques form a **Unique Quarter**. Only **worked** rural tiles
  yield. No loyalty mechanic — settle freely.

## Yields

| Yield | Use |
|---|---|
| Food | Growth (new population). Towns export surplus. Not hit by unhappiness. |
| Production | Cities only — builds everything. Towns convert it to Gold. |
| Gold | Purchases, upkeep, Town→City conversion. |
| Science | Tech tree. Build the Age's science buildings; pair them for adjacency. |
| Culture | Civics tree. |
| Happiness | Per-settlement; surplus → empire Celebrations (extra policy slot + bonus). Negative happiness cuts non-food yields hard. |
| Influence | Diplomacy currency: befriend Independent Powers, endeavors, sanctions, espionage. Spend it. |

- **Specialists** (assign pop to urban tiles) give Science+Culture but cost Food
  + Happiness — don't over-assign into negative yields.
- **Resources** slot into settlements (Bonus = anywhere; City = Cities only,
  stronger; Empire = passive). Reassign when you gain a resource or slot.

## Units & Commanders

- **Commanders** are the core mechanic and are **Ageless** (gain XP/promotions,
  carry across Ages). They **pack** several units into one stack (+1 movement
  when carrying units), deploy them at the front, and request reinforcements.
  Promote toward your military need (defense/offense/movement/army-size).
- Combat auto-resolves with front/flank bonuses. **Walls** add big defensive HP
  +CS. Each fortified urban district must be captured separately to take a city.
- Civilians: Settlers/Founders found towns (capital can produce Settlers around
  pop 5). Scouts explore (use their Search/explore ops to reveal huts + meet
  Independent Powers).
- Age-specific military units are **deleted at the Age transition** — don't
  over-invest in them late in an Age.

## Early game (first ~30 turns)

1. **Found the capital immediately** (turn 1) on/near fresh water with good Food;
   don't wander for a "perfect" tile.
2. Build **Scouts first** (1–2) and explore outward; meet Independent Powers and
   find Discoveries.
3. First research follows terrain (pastures→Animal Husbandry, coastal→Sailing,
   etc.); first civic → a good Pantheon + first Government.
4. After scouts: Granary/economy → **Settlers** → expand to claim resource/river
   sites before rivals, staying at/under the cap.
5. Bank and spend **Influence**; begin befriending a well-placed Independent
   Power (also feeds the Military path).
6. **Lock a Legacy Path by ~turn 15–20** and bend production/research/civics
   toward its milestones.

## Pitfalls

- Overshooting the settlement cap (empire-wide happiness penalty).
- Hoarding Influence / Gold / Attribute points — spend them every turn.
- Over-specializing into negative Gold/Happiness.
- Building age-specific units right before a transition (they vanish).
- Treating it as one continuous game — plan around the leader + Commanders +
  Wonders + Traditions (the only things that carry over).
- Ignoring Legacy Paths — failing a path's milestones gives a Dark Age penalty.
- Forgetting the Modern Age Score-Victory clock.
