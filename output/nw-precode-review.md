**Date:** 2026-06-18

## Verdict: blocked

The change is conceptually sound and the live-proof discipline is excellent (engine-as-legality-authority, RNG-free determinism, live multi-seed closure gate, probe-before-rely ordering for FOUR*/cliff geometry all hold up). It is **blocked** purely on **design/tasks TEXT** that instructs the implementer to do the wrong thing in three places. None of these are conceptual flaws — they are factually incorrect descriptions of the current code that, if implemented literally, would (a) break wonders that work today, (b) leave the parity bug masked, or (c) mis-scope a live contract refactor as dead-code deletion. Fix the text, then implement.

**Counts:** P1 = 3 · P2 = 4 · P3 = 6

## Recommended dispositions

1. **Block on the three P1 design-TEXT corrections (NW-1, NW-2, NW-3)** and the variety-mechanism P1 (NW-4). These are pre-code: rewrite design §3/§6 and Tasks 2.1/2.2/2.4/4.x so the implementer is not told to (a) re-key offsets to the wrong order / import across a boundary, (b) "unmask" a mock that already shares the function, (c) "delete" a live contract field, or (d) ship a seed-invariant selection loop labeled as variety-producing.
2. **Resolve NW-7 (planner footprint source) before NW-3's migration is coded** — the two are coupled; pick the mapgen-core-primitive path to also defuse the boundary half of NW-1.
3. **Fold the table-sync test (NW-1) and the 4th-copy consolidation (NW-13) into one cross-package equality test.**
4. **Fold NW-4 and NW-5 into a single per-wonder re-ranking work item** — they are the same defect seen from product and adversarial angles; the `priority` output must remain `[0,1]` and emit per-wonder suitability.
5. **P2 NW-6/NW-8** are duplicated-logic / second-site hazards: de-duplicate the support filter and pin the FOUR* direction-normalization behavior before close.
6. **P3s** are docs/test-robustness/weight-tuning refinements — address before close but they do not gate the start of implementation.
